02 Algorithms

16 dithering algorithms, each with its own visual character.

Error Diffusion

These algorithms scan the image pixel-by-pixel, quantize each pixel to the nearest palette color, then spread the quantization error to neighboring pixels.

Floyd-Steinberg
The classic. Balanced 2×3 kernel diffusing to 4 neighbors. Good all-rounder.
Atkinson
Mac classic look. Only diffuses 3/4 of the error — gives higher contrast, distinctive style.
Burkes
Smoother gradients than Floyd-Steinberg. 2×5 kernel, 7 neighbors.
Jarvis-Judice-Ninke
Large 3×5 kernel, 12 neighbors. Smoothest error diffusion but slowest.
Stucki
Similar to JJN with slightly different weights. Very smooth results.
Sierra (3 variants)
Full Sierra, Two-Row, and Lite. Ranging from smooth to fast, good balance of quality.

Ordered Dither (Bayer)

Uses a threshold matrix tiled across the image. Each pixel is compared against its matrix position to decide on/off. No error diffusion — very fast, distinctive crosshatch pattern.

VariantMatrixCharacter
Bayer 2×24 levelsCoarse, chunky
Bayer 4×416 levelsClassic retro
Bayer 8×864 levelsSmooth gradients
Bayer 16×16256 levelsVery smooth

Other Methods

AlgorithmHow It Works
ThresholdSimple binary cut at adjustable level. No dithering — just on or off.
Random NoiseRandom threshold per pixel. Grainy, film-like.
Blue NoisePre-computed 64×64 blue noise matrix. Organic, even distribution without visible patterns.
HalftoneCircular dot pattern with adjustable size and angle. Newspaper / print look.

04 Camera & Capture

Live dithering from your device camera, photo snaps, and video recording.

Live Preview

Tap Camera in the toolbar to start your camera. The feed runs through the full dithering pipeline at ~30fps. Change any setting — algorithm, palette, brightness — and see it update instantly.

If your device has multiple cameras, a dropdown selector appears in the toolbar to switch between them.

Snap Photos

Tap the round snap button (or press Space) to capture the current dithered output. A thumbnail slides to the bottom-left corner onto a photo stack — no dialogs, no interruption.

Record Video Loops

Long-press the snap button (hold for 500ms) to start recording. A red ring fills around the button showing progress. Release to stop, or it auto-stops at the configured duration (1–12 seconds).

The video records from the dithered output canvas at the current export scale, and auto-downloads as MP4 or WebM.

Photo Gallery

Tap the photo stack in the bottom-left to open the gallery. From here you can:

  • Tap a capture to re-load it as the source image
  • Delete captures with the × button
  • Export All to download every capture as PNG

05 Particle Transport

Experimental mass-preserving particle physics mode.

When transport is enabled, the dithered "on" pixels become physical particles with position and velocity. Instead of instantly showing the dithered result, particles must flow to their target positions. The total count of active particles is conserved.

Physics Modes

Overdamped
Smooth slide to targets. No oscillation. Like iron filings pulled by a magnet.
Underdamped
Spring-mass with low damping. Particles overshoot and bounce before settling. Jelly-like.
Ballistic
Particles launched toward targets then decelerate. Explosive scatter-then-settle.
Diffusion
Random walk biased toward target. Organic, Brownian motion. Cloud-like convergence.

Transport Controls

ControlRangeEffect
Spring (k)0.01 – 2.0How strongly particles are pulled toward targets
Damping0.01 – 1.0Velocity friction. Higher = faster settling.
Repulsion0.0 – 2.0Short-range push between nearby particles
Mass0.1 – 5.0Particle inertia. Heavier = slower response.
Max Velocity1 – 20 px/frameSpeed cap to prevent particles flying off
Show Trailson / offRender fading trail behind each particle
Trail Length1 – 20 framesHow long the trail persists
Mass Lockon / offAuto-adjust brightness to maintain exact particle count

Assignment Methods

MethodSpeedQuality
Greedy NearestFastEach particle claims its nearest available target. Good enough for most use cases.
KD-TreeFastSpatial tree for better nearest-neighbor lookup at scale.
RandomInstantRandom assignment creates maximum visual chaos during transport.

Initialization Modes

When transport activates, particles need starting positions:

  • Previous Frame — particles carry over from last frame (best for camera/video)
  • Random — scattered randomly, then flow to targets
  • Center Burst — all start at center, explode outward
  • Edge Rain — particles enter from top and fall to targets

Resolution

Pixel Scale (1×–64×) controls how many input pixels map to one dithered pixel. At 16×, a 640×480 input becomes 40×30 working resolution — big chunky pixels.

Or pick an exact resolution from the presets: Game Boy 160×144, NES 256×240, PICO-8 128×128, etc.

Output always renders with image-rendering: pixelated — hard pixel edges, no blurring.

User Presets

Click Save Preset to store your current settings with a name. Presets are saved in your browser's localStorage and persist across sessions. You can also export all your presets as a JSON file and import them on another device.

URL Sharing

Every setting is automatically encoded in the URL hash. Copy the URL and share it — anyone who opens it gets the exact same algorithm, palette, brightness, contrast, etc.

Example: ...#a=atkinson&p=PICO-8&s=8&b=-10&c=20

The source image is not included in the URL — just the settings.

10 Technical Details

For the curious.

  • Zero dependencies — vanilla HTML + CSS + JS, no framework, no build step
  • Web Worker dithering — all 16 algorithms run off the main thread
  • Transferable ArrayBuffers — zero-copy frame passing between threads
  • 32×32×32 RGB LUT — nearest palette color lookup is O(1) per pixel
  • Spatial hash grid — O(1) neighbor queries for transport particle collisions
  • PWA — works offline after first load via service worker
  • ~200KB total — all code, no external assets

Browser Support

Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari — desktop and mobile. Camera features require HTTPS or localhost. MediaRecorder for video export may fall back to WebM on some browsers.
